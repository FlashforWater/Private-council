# Research Foundations

Private Council draws from several bodies of work. The current implementation is a product prototype, not a formal research system, but the design is influenced by these methods.

## Structured Expert Deliberation

- Delphi Method
- Nominal Group Technique
- Facilitated group process

Product implication: generate independent views before synthesis and preserve disagreement.

## Argument Mapping

- Toulmin argument model
- IBIS / Issue-Based Information System
- Argument mapping for deliberation

Product implication: structure claims, assumptions, evidence, objections, and risks instead of saving only chat logs.

## Decision Science

- Decision analysis
- Multi-criteria decision analysis
- Analytic Hierarchy Process
- Weighted decision matrices

Product implication: evaluate options through criteria, weights, tradeoffs, and sensitivity rather than asking for a generic "best" answer.

## Voting and Social Choice

- Arrow's impossibility theorem
- Approval voting
- Ranked and scored aggregation methods

Product implication: role preferences are signals, not automatic verdicts.

## Bias, Groupthink, and Red Teaming

- Groupthink research
- Premortem analysis
- Red-team methods
- Analysis of competing hypotheses

Product implication: protect dissent and challenge assumptions before recommendation.

## Forecasting and Calibration

- Brier score
- Calibration
- Forecasting tournaments
- Superforecasting

Product implication: record predictions and score them later so the system can learn which roles and models are useful.

## LLM Multi-agent and Cross-validation Research

- Multi-agent debate
- Self-consistency
- Tree of Thoughts
- Adaptive debate / debate only when necessary
- Multi-agent consensus and roundtable-style systems

Product implication: use multiple roles or models selectively, especially when disagreement, uncertainty, or risk is high.
